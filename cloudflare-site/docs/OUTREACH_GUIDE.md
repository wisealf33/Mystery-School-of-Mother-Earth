# Vendor And Practitioner Outreach Guide

## Who To Contact First

- Herbalists
- Gardeners and growers
- Natural product makers
- Energy workers and healing arts practitioners
- Yoga, meditation, and spiritual teachers
- Homesteading and preparedness educators
- Green cleaners, landscapers, and ecological businesses
- Handmade goods vendors
- Farmers market vendors
- Local wellness practitioners
- Community organizers
- Event hosts and venue owners

## Outreach Message

Hi [Name], I am gathering founding interest for Mystery School of Mother Earth Foundation, an emerging private membership community for earth stewardship, natural living, local resilience, green business, practical earth skills, healing arts, spiritual growth, and real community.

The first stage is online: a weekly newsletter, member directory, and pathways for vendors, practitioners, teachers, makers, growers, and circle-builders to be promoted inside the association. The first local chapter is likely to grow around Peotone, Kankakee, Wilmington, and the southern South Chicagoland area, with local events as an early chapter model.

I thought of you because of your work with [specific reason]. Would you like to be on the founding interest list or hear about the vendor/practitioner pathway when it opens?

## Intake Questions

- Name
- Email
- Phone
- Location
- Website or social links
- What do you offer?
- Which categories fit you?
- Are you interested in directory placement?
- Are you interested in newsletter features?
- Are you interested in Mother Earth Market participation?
- Are you interested in teaching a class or workshop?
- Are you interested in helping form a local circle?
- Are you already a basic member?

## First Outreach Goal

Do not try to sell everything at once. The first goal is to identify 25 to 50 aligned people who want updates and may become founding members, vendors, practitioners, teachers, or circle-builders.

## Follow-Up Rhythm

- Day 1: Personal invitation
- Day 4: Gentle follow-up
- Day 10: Send project intro and founding list link
- Weekly: Newsletter keeps them warm
- Monthly: Invite deeper participation
