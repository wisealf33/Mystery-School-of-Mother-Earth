# Weekly Newsletter Plan

## Purpose

The newsletter is a now-stage product. It should make the foundation feel alive before the full membership platform exists.

It should:

- Promote members, vendors, practitioners, and green businesses
- Teach practical earth skills
- Announce circles, classes, markets, and member opportunities
- Share community stories
- Invite people to join or upgrade
- Keep the project moving every week

## Suggested Name

**Mother Earth Weekly**

Alternate names:

- The Mother Earth Dispatch
- Circle Notes
- The Living Earth Letter

## Recurring Sections

- Opening note from the founder
- Member spotlight
- Vendor/practitioner feature
- Earth skill of the week
- Garden / seasonal note
- Circle-builder update
- Mother Earth Market planning note
- Community asks and offers
- Join / upgrade / submit an offering

## First Four Issues

**Issue 1: The Foundation Begins**

- Introduce the project
- Explain online-first umbrella
- Invite founding members
- Ask vendors/practitioners to raise their hands

**Issue 2: What We Can Build Together**

- Explain directory and newsletter promotion
- Ask people what they offer
- Invite Chapter 001 interest around Peotone, Kankakee, Wilmington, and southern South Chicagoland

**Issue 3: Mother Earth Circles**

- Explain local circles
- Ask for circle-builders, hosts, volunteers, and teachers
- Share early local project ideas

**Issue 4: Mother Earth Market**

- Introduce market model
- Explain Spirit Bucks
- Invite vendors/practitioners to apply
- Ask for possible venues and local partners

## Tools To Consider

Start with the tool that feels easiest to actually use weekly:

- MailerLite: simple landing pages, forms, automations, affordable
- ConvertKit: creator-friendly tags and sequences
- Beehiiv: publication-first newsletter
- Flodesk: visual emails, simple forms
- Mailchimp: broad familiarity, more complex over time

For this project, MailerLite or ConvertKit is likely enough for the first launch.
