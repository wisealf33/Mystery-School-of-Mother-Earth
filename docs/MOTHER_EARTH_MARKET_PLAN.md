# Mother Earth Market Plan

## Purpose

Mother Earth Market is a member-centered event model that can launch the first local circle and become repeatable for future circles.

## Event Concept

A local gathering featuring member vendors, practitioners, growers, makers, teachers, and community projects connected to natural living, earth stewardship, green business, practical skills, healing arts, and spiritual growth.

## Spirit Bucks

Current concept:

- Attendee pays $5 entry.
- Attendee receives five $1 Spirit Bucks.
- Each Spirit Buck can be used as $1 off one vendor transaction of $10 or more.
- One Spirit Buck per qualifying transaction.
- Spirit Bucks have no cash value.
- Spirit Bucks are not reimbursed by the organizer.
- Vendors keep 100% of their sales.
- Entry funds support the event and association.

Use simple signage so attendees and vendors understand the rules quickly.

## First Market Roles

- Event lead
- Vendor coordinator
- Check-in table
- Spirit Bucks table
- Setup crew
- Breakdown crew
- Workshop/demo coordinator
- Photographer/content helper
- Safety/logistics helper
- Newsletter/social media helper

## Vendor Categories

- Growers and gardeners
- Herbal goods
- Natural body and home products
- Handmade goods
- Healing arts practitioners
- Green businesses
- Teachers and workshop hosts
- Local resilience projects
- Food preservation and practical skill educators

## First Venue Criteria

- Easy parking
- Restrooms
- Indoor backup or weather plan
- Room for vendor tables
- Table/chair access or rental plan
- Clear entry/check-in area
- Permission for sales and workshops
- Insurance/liability requirements understood

## Launch Timeline

**8 weeks out:** choose venue, draft vendor invitation, open founding vendor interest.

**6 weeks out:** confirm first vendor list, publish save-the-date, recruit volunteers.

**4 weeks out:** promote weekly, gather workshop/demo ideas, prepare Spirit Bucks materials.

**2 weeks out:** final vendor map, signage, check-in flow, newsletter push.

**Event week:** daily social reminders, vendor confirmations, print materials.

**After event:** thank attendees, feature vendors, collect feedback, publish photos, invite people into membership.
