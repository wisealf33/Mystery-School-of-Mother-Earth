# Project Blueprint

## Core Identity

**Mystery School of Mother Earth Foundation** is a private membership association and online-first ecosystem for earth stewardship, natural living, local resilience, green business, gardening, practical earth skills, healing arts, spiritual growth, mystery-school learning, and real community.

Public language should lead with grounded earth-centered benefits, then invite people into the deeper healing, spiritual, and mystery-school dimensions.

## Strategic Shape

Build the online umbrella now. Use it to attract founding members, vendors, practitioners, supporters, teachers, circle-builders, and local organizers. Promote members through the newsletter, directory, website, Facebook channels, and future member opportunities. Then form the first local Mother Earth Circle through Chapter 001, focused on the southern South Chicagoland area around Peotone, Kankakee, Wilmington, and nearby south/southwest communities.

## Product Layers

**Public Front Door**

- Mission and vision
- Basic member invitation
- General benefits
- Founding interest form
- Newsletter signup
- Public-facing explanation of circles and markets

**Private Member Association Layer**

- Basic membership
- Member agreements and private terms
- Member opportunities
- Internal event and vendor participation details
- Member-only newsletter details, offers, promotions, and contribution schedules

**Member Promotion Layer**

- Directory
- Newsletter features
- Facebook spotlights
- Website placements
- Event listings
- Workshop/class submissions

**Local Circle Layer**

- Circle-builder intake
- Local host guide
- Monthly gathering format
- Volunteer roles
- Local project ideas
- Market planning path

**Mother Earth Market Layer**

- Vendor/practitioner application
- Event page
- Spirit Bucks rules
- Attendee entry flow
- Vendor promotion package
- Post-event reporting

## First-Version Priorities

1. Make the project understandable in under one minute.
2. Collect emails and interest types.
3. Identify founding vendors and practitioners.
4. Publish weekly newsletter content quickly.
5. Seed the Facebook page and group.
6. Start conversations with local circle-builders around Peotone, Kankakee, Wilmington, and the southern South Chicagoland area.
7. Validate the first market format before overbuilding technology.

## What Not To Overbuild Yet

- Full custom member login
- Complex marketplace transactions
- Automated vendor payouts
- Chapter governance software
- Public event ticketing
- Large course platform

Those can come later after the founding community proves what it actually uses.
