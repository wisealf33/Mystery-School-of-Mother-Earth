# Advertising And Flyer Concepts

## Core Public Message

Start with grounded language:

**Natural living. Local resilience. Earth-centered community.**

Then expand:

Mystery School of Mother Earth Foundation is gathering founding members, vendors, practitioners, growers, makers, teachers, and circle-builders for an online-first private membership community rooted in earth stewardship, practical skills, healing arts, spiritual growth, and real community.

## Founding Member Flyer Copy

**Mystery School of Mother Earth Foundation**

Natural living. Local resilience. Earth-centered community.

Now gathering founding interest for a private membership community centered around earth stewardship, gardening, green business, practical earth skills, healing arts, spiritual growth, and real community.

Join the founding interest list to receive updates about membership, the weekly newsletter, member directory, vendor/practitioner pathways, Mother Earth Circles, and the future Mother Earth Market.

First local chapter seed: Chapter 001, focused around Peotone, Kankakee, Wilmington, and the southern South Chicagoland area.

## Vendor / Practitioner Flyer Copy

**Calling growers, makers, healers, teachers, practitioners, and green businesses**

Mystery School of Mother Earth Foundation is building an online-first private membership ecosystem with future opportunities for member directory placement, newsletter features, website spotlights, classes, workshops, local circles, and Mother Earth Market participation.

If your work supports natural living, earth stewardship, practical skills, healing arts, green business, spiritual growth, or real community, join the founding interest list.

## Simple Flyer Design Direction

- Use warm natural photography, garden/market/community imagery
- Keep the title large and readable
- Include one clear call to action
- Add QR code to founding interest form
- Avoid cluttered spiritual imagery in public flyers
- Let the deeper mystery-school language appear in supporting copy, not as the only hook
